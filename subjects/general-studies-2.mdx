# General Studies 2: Evaluation Framework

GS2 covers Polity & Constitution, Governance, Social Justice, and International Relations. The evaluator emphasizes Constitutional Accuracy, Administrative Pragmatism, and Strategic Depth.

## Mark Allocation

- **10-mark questions**: Max ceiling 7/10 (70%)
- **15-mark questions**: Max ceiling 10.5/15 (70%)
- **20-mark questions**: Max ceiling 14/20 (70%)

<Note>
GS2 requires high technical density with specific Articles, Case Laws, Committees, and Strategic Doctrines. Generic answers are capped at 30%.
</Note>

## The Four Sections

### Section 1: Polity & Constitution

**Topics Covered:**
- Indian Constitution: Historical underpinnings, features, amendments, significant provisions, basic structure
- Federal structure: Union-State relations, devolution of powers, dispute redressal
- Separation of powers and institutions
- Parliament and State Legislatures: Structure, functioning, powers & privileges
- Executive and Judiciary: Structure, functioning, appointment, reforms
- Statutory, regulatory and quasi-judicial bodies
- Representation of People's Act, electoral reforms

**Non-Negotiables (6 marks total):**

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Article Density (2 marks)">
    **Must cite specific Articles:**
    - Art. 131 for Federalism disputes
    - Art. 142 for Judicial Activism (complete justice)
    - Art. 356 for President's Rule
    - Art. 102/191 for Disqualification
    - Art. 280 for Finance Commission
    - Art. 153-167 for Governor's office
    
    <Warning>
    Vague references like "constitutional provisions" without Article numbers = Low marks
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Landmark Judgments (2 marks)">
    **Must cite specific cases:**
    - **Kesavananda Bharati**: Basic Structure Doctrine
    - **S.R. Bommai**: Federalism, Art. 356 safeguards
    - **Lily Thomas**: RPA, criminal legislators
    - **I.R. Coelho**: 9th Schedule judicial review
    - **Kihoto Hollohan**: 10th Schedule anti-defection
    - **Nabam Rebia**: Governor's discretion
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="RPA & Statutory Framework (1 mark)">
    - Section 8(3) of RPA 1951 (disqualification on conviction)
    - Section 123 of RPA 1951 (corrupt practices)
    - Dinesh Goswami Committee on electoral reforms
    - Vohra Committee on criminalization of politics
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Constitutional Morality Vocabulary (1 mark)">
    **Technical terms:**
    - Asymmetrical Federalism
    - Separation of Powers vs Checks and Balances
    - Unitary Bias in Indian federalism
    - Procedure Established by Law vs Due Process
    - Doctrine of Essentiality
    - Basic Structure Doctrine
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

**PESTEL Dimensions:**

- **Political**: Power-sharing (Cooperative vs Competitive Federalism), Governor's role, Anti-defection law
- **Economic/Fiscal**: Finance Commission (Art. 280), Cess/Surcharge issues, GST compensation disputes
- **Social**: Fundamental Rights for marginalized (Art. 15/17), Reservation policies, Minority rights
- **Technological**: e-Courts, e-Vidhaan, Digital Parliament
- **Environmental**: Environmental Federalism, Inter-state water disputes, NGT/CAQM roles
- **Legal**: Judicial review, PIL evolution, Tribunalization of justice

**Scoring Matrix:**

| Range | Marks (10) | Marks (15) | Description |
|-------|------------|------------|-------------|
| Poor | 0-3 | 0-4.5 | Vague points, "Social Studies" style, no Articles, no Case Laws |

## Key Evaluation Criteria

### IC5 Structure for GS2

All answers must follow this enhanced framework:

**I (Introduction):**
- Constitutional Anchor or Current High-Value Context

**C (Context):**
- PESTEL lenses applied to the topic

**C (Current Relevance):**
- PRS Legislative Research data or NITI Aayog indices

**C (Content):**
- Multi-layered analysis with specific Articles/Cases/Committees

**C (Conclusion):**
- Link to SDG 16 (Peace, Justice, Strong Institutions)
- Visionary Quote (Ambedkar/Gandhi/Preamble)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

<Warning>
**Generic Penalty**: Correct but lacking Non-Negotiables (Articles/Cases/Data) caps at 30% (3/10 or 4.5/15).
</Warning>

1. **Vague constitutional references**: "Constitution says" instead of citing Article numbers
2. **No case law citations**: Missing landmark judgments
3. **Ignoring committee reports**: Not citing 2nd ARC, Sachar, Xaxa by name
4. **Missing maps in IR**: No spatial representation for regional questions
5. **Generic news reporting**: Not using strategic terminology (De-hyphenation, Multi-alignment)
6. **No data/statistics**: Missing NFHS-5, PLFS, NCRB figures

## Topper Benchmarks

**High-Scoring Answers Include:**

1. **Institutional Hierarchy Diagrams**: 10th Schedule defection flow, Collegium appointment process
2. **Comparison Tables**: Indian vs US/UK Secularism, Nagara vs Dravida temples
3. **PRS Legislative Research data**: Parliamentary efficiency statistics
4. **Specific committee recommendations**: With report numbers (e.g., 2nd ARC 4th Report)
5. **Regional maps with choke points**: For IR questions
6. **Quotes from visionaries**: S. Jaishankar, Vijayalakshmi Pandit, Ambedkar
