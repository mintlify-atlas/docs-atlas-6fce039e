The JKPSC Answer Evaluator automatically saves all your evaluations locally, allowing you to track improvement over time and review past performance.

## Recent Evaluations Section

Every evaluation is saved in the **"Recent Evaluations"** history section:

<img src="/images/history-section.png" alt="Recent evaluations list showing past submissions" />

### What You'll See

Each saved evaluation displays:

- **Score badge:** Your score with color coding (green/orange/red)
- **Subject tag:** The subject you selected (GS1, GS2, GS3, GS4, or Essay)
- **Timestamp:** How long ago the evaluation was completed
- **Question preview:** Truncated view of the question text

### Viewing Past Evaluations

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the evaluation">
    Scroll through your Recent Evaluations list. By default, only the 3 most recent evaluations are shown.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Expand the list (if needed)">
    If you have more than 3 evaluations, click **"Show X more"** to expand the full list.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click the eye icon">
    Hover over any evaluation to reveal action buttons, then click the **eye icon** to view the full evaluation results.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The complete evaluation (all categories, feedback, tips, and model answer) will be displayed below, exactly as it appeared when first generated.

<Tip>
  You can re-download the PDF report for any past evaluation by viewing it and clicking **"Download PDF Report"**.
</Tip>

### Deleting Individual Evaluations

To remove a specific evaluation:

1. Hover over the evaluation entry
2. Click the **trash icon** (red on hover)
3. The evaluation is immediately deleted from local storage

<Warning>
  Deletions are permanent and cannot be undone. Consider exporting your history before deleting important evaluations.
</Warning>

## Progress Dashboard

When you have **at least 2 evaluations**, the **Progress Dashboard** automatically appears:

<img src="/images/progress-dashboard.png" alt="Progress dashboard with trend charts" />

### Dashboard Controls

The dashboard has two main views:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Score Trend (Line Chart)">
    Shows your percentage scores over time, with each point representing one evaluation.
    
    **What it shows:**
    - All evaluations in chronological order (oldest → newest)
    - Separate colored lines for each subject (GS1, GS2, GS3, GS4, Essay)
    - A **70% reference line** indicating the scoring ceiling
    - X-axis: Date labels
    - Y-axis: Percentage score (0-100%)
    
    **How to interpret:**
    - Look for upward trends in specific subjects
    - Scores clustering near 70% indicate strong consistent performance
    - Dips may indicate harder topics or knowledge gaps
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="By Subject (Bar Chart)">
    Compares your **average** and **best** scores across subjects.
    
    **What it shows:**
    - One bar group per subject
    - Purple bar: Average score across all attempts
    - Green bar: Best score achieved in that subject
    - Number of attempts per subject
    
    **How to interpret:**
    - Subjects with large gaps between average and best need consistency work
    - Low average scores indicate subjects requiring more study/practice
    - High best scores show your potential in that subject
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Expanding and Collapsing the Dashboard

By default, the dashboard may be collapsed to save space:

## The 70% Reference Line

Both chart views include a **70% reference line**:

<Note>
  **Why 70%?**
  
  In UPSC/JKPSC mains evaluations, scoring above 70% is extremely rare and indicates exceptional performance. This reference line helps you set realistic expectations:
  
  - **60-70%:** Very good performance, competitive score
  - **50-60%:** Good attempt with room for improvement
  - **Below 50%:** Needs significant work
  
  Consistently scoring 60%+ across subjects is an excellent goal.
</Note>

## Exporting Your History

You can export all your evaluations as a JSON file for backup or sharing:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Export">
    In the Recent Evaluations header, click the **"Export"** button (download icon).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save the file">
    A JSON file named `jkpscprep-history-YYYY-MM-DD.json` will be downloaded to your device.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Store safely">
    Keep this file as a backup or share it with mentors for review.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### What's in the Export File?

The JSON file contains:

- All evaluation results (scores, category breakdowns, feedback)
- Question and answer text for each evaluation
- Subject, marks, and timestamps
- Complete metadata for recreation

<Accordion title="Sample export structure">
  ```json
  [
    {
      "id": "eval_1234567890123",
      "timestamp": 1234567890123,
      "subject": "GS2",
      "marks": 10,
      "question": "Critically examine...",
      "result": {
        "overallScore": 7.5,
        "maxScore": 10,
        "categories": [...],
        "keyPointsAnalysis": {...},
        "improvementTips": [...],
        "modelAnswerStructure": "...",
        "strengths": [...],
        "summary": "..."
      },
      "score": 7.5,
      "maxScore": 10,
      "percentage": 75
    }
  ]
  ```
</Accordion>

## Importing History

You can import previously exported history files:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Import">
    In the Recent Evaluations header, click the **"Import"** button (upload icon).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select your JSON file">
    Choose a previously exported `.json` history file from your device.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Choose import mode">
    A dialog will appear with two options:
    
    **Merge** — Adds imported evaluations to your existing history (recommended)
    
    **Replace All** — Deletes current history and replaces it with imported data
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm">
    Click your chosen option. The evaluations will be loaded immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  **Replace All is permanent!**
  
  If you choose "Replace All", your current evaluation history will be deleted and cannot be recovered. Always export your current history first before replacing.
</Warning>

### When to Import/Export

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Switching devices">
    Export on Device A → Import on Device B to access your history anywhere.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Sharing with mentors">
    Export your history and share the JSON file with teachers/mentors for review.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Backup before clearing browser data">
    Since history is stored in browser local storage, clearing browser data will delete it. Export first!
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Archiving old evaluations">
    Export and clear history periodically to keep the app fast (e.g., after each exam cycle).
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Clearing All History

To delete all saved evaluations at once:

1. Click the **"Clear All"** button in the Recent Evaluations header
2. Confirm the deletion (this action cannot be undone)
3. All evaluations and progress data will be permanently removed

<Tip>
  **Before clearing all history:**
  
  Always export your data first! You might want to review these evaluations later or share them with mentors.
</Tip>

## Understanding Progress Patterns

### Healthy Progress Indicators

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Upward Trend" icon="trending-up">
    Your score trend line slopes upward over time, showing consistent improvement.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Reduced Variation" icon="chart-line">
    Scores become more consistent (less fluctuation) as you develop skills.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Category Balance" icon="balance-scale">
    You're scoring similarly across all evaluation categories (no major weak spots).
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Subject Parity" icon="equals">
    Average scores are similar across subjects (GS1-4), indicating well-rounded preparation.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Red Flags to Address

<Warning>
  Watch for these patterns that indicate areas needing focus:
  
  - **Declining trend:** Scores dropping over time (may indicate burnout or wrong approach)
  - **High variation:** Wild swings between high and low scores (inconsistent preparation)
  - **Subject gaps:** One subject consistently scoring 15-20% lower than others
  - **Plateauing:** Scores stuck at the same level for 5+ evaluations (need strategy change)
</Warning>

## Using Progress Data to Improve

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review every 5 evaluations">
    After every 5 evaluations, spend time analyzing the Progress Dashboard to identify trends.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Compare category feedback">
    Look at recent evaluations side-by-side — are you getting similar feedback repeatedly? That's your improvement priority.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Set subject-specific goals">
    If GS3 scores are consistently lower, dedicate more study time to technology/economy topics.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Track improvement tip implementation">
    Keep a notebook of improvement tips you've received and consciously implement 1-2 in each new answer.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Celebrate milestones">
    Hit 65%+ consistently? Crossed 70% for the first time? Acknowledge these achievements to stay motivated.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Next Steps

Now that you understand progress tracking:
- Aim for consistent 60%+ scores across subjects
- Review past evaluations to identify recurring feedback
- Export your history regularly as backup
- Use the Progress Dashboard to guide your study priorities
