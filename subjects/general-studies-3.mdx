# General Studies 3: Evaluation Framework

GS3 covers Economy & Agriculture, Science & Technology, Environment & Disaster Management, and Internal Security. The evaluator emphasizes Macro-Financial Accuracy, Mechanism Explanation, Framework Anchoring, and Strategic Depth.

## Mark Allocation

- **10-mark questions**: Max ceiling 7/10 (70%)
- **15-mark questions**: Max ceiling 10.5/15 (70%)
- **20-mark questions**: Max ceiling 14/20 (70%)

<Note>
GS3 requires high data density with specific Economic Survey figures, Committee targets, Global Framework compliance (COP, Sendai, FATF), and labeled technical diagrams.
</Note>

## The Four Sections

### Section 1: Economy & Agriculture

**Topics Covered:**
- Indian Economy: Growth, Development, Employment, Inclusive Growth
- Government Budgeting: Fiscal Policy, Monetary Policy, Taxation
- Major crops, cropping patterns, irrigation, storage/transport/marketing
- Direct/indirect farm subsidies and MSP
- Food processing and related industries
- Land reforms in India
- Liberalization effects, industrial policy changes
- Infrastructure: Energy, Ports, Roads, Airports, Railways
- Investment models: PPP, FDI

## Key Evaluation Criteria

### IC5 Structure for GS3

**I (Introduction):**
- Technical Definition, Current Economic/Security Context, or Recent Disaster/Event with data

**C (Context):**
- PESTEL lenses applied with specific data and frameworks

**C (Current Relevance):**
- Link to recent trends (De-dollarization, Generative AI, GLOF, Deepfakes)

**C (Content):**
- Multi-layered analysis with committee targets, framework compliance

**C (Conclusion):**
- Way Forward linked to Vision 2047 (Viksit Bharat) or specific SDG target

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

<Warning>
**Generic Penalty**: Correct but lacking Non-Negotiables (Data/Frameworks/Terms) caps at 30% (3/10 or 4.5/15).
</Warning>

1. **Generic economic talk**: "Poverty is bad" without citing MPI, Oxfam data, or committee recommendations
2. **No mechanism explanation**: Just naming technologies without explaining "how it works"
3. **Missing framework anchoring**: Not citing Sendai, COP26, FATF by name
4. **No labeled diagrams**: Missing mechanism sketches, hazard maps, or security maps
5. **Ignoring committee targets**: Not citing N.K. Singh's 50% debt-to-GDP by 2030-31 or Swaminathan's C2+50%
6. **No external linkages**: Not connecting internal security to external state actors

## Topper Benchmarks

**High-Scoring Answers Include:**

1. **Economic Graphs**: Laffer Curve, Kuznets Curve, J-Curve with proper labels
2. **India Maps**: Agro-climatic zones, Seismic zones, Red Corridor, IOR Choke Points
3. **Mechanism Sketches**: How CAR-T works, Blockchain layers, Earthquake wave propagation
4. **Committee Targets with Numbers**: N.K. Singh (50% debt-to-GDP by 2030-31), Swaminathan (C2+50%)
5. **Framework Compliance**: Sendai 4 Priorities + 7 Targets, COP26 Panchamrit pledges
6. **Value-Chain Analysis**: Farm-to-Fork, Money Laundering Triad, Sendai DM Cycle
