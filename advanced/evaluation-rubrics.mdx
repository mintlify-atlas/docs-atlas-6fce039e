## Overview

The JKPSC Answer Evaluator uses sophisticated, subject-specific rubrics aligned with UPSC topper benchmarks. Each subject (GS1-4, Essay) has dedicated evaluation criteria that mirror real UPSC marking standards.

## Rubric Architecture

Every rubric consists of:

- **Non-Negotiables**: Mandatory checkpoints (Articles, Cases, Data, Technical Terms)
- **PESTEL+ Dimensions**: Multi-dimensional analytical frameworks
- **Scoring Matrix**: Band-wise descriptions (Poor → Average → Good → Topper)
- **Max Ceiling**: Subject-specific 70% cap (mirrors UPSC topper scores of 60-65%)
- **Topper Markers**: High-value elements that distinguish exceptional answers

## GS1 Rubrics

### Block-Based Evaluation

GS1 is divided into 5 blocks, each with specialized rubrics:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Art & Culture">
    **Syllabus Coverage:**
    - Indian Art Forms (Paintings, Dances, Music, Puppetry, Pottery)
    - Visual Arts & Sculpture (Harappan to Modern)
    - Architecture (Temple styles, Cave, Indo-Islamic)
    - Literature (Vedic, Sanskrit, Sangam, Medieval)
    - Bhakti & Sufi Movements

    **Non-Negotiables (6 marks total):**
    - **Technical Terminology (2 marks)**: Garbhagriha, Mandapa, Shikhara, Pietra Dura, Shadanga, Fresco-buono vs Fresco-secco
    - **Visual Representation (2 marks)**: Labeled sketch, Stupa cross-section, Temple floor plan, India map of art forms
    - **Chronological Context (1 mark)**: Dynasty/ruler, Material used (Red Sandstone-Mathura, Schist-Gandhara)
    - **Textual Sources (1 mark)**: Natyashastra, Vishnudharmottara Purana, Silappadikaram

    **PESTEL Dimensions:**
    - Socio-Religious: Hinayana to Mahayana transition, Bhakti influence
    - Political: Art as state propaganda (Ashokan Pillars)
    - Economic: Patronage sources (Guilds, Merchants)
    - Environmental: Local materials (Terracotta in Bengal, Stone in Odisha)

    **Scoring Ceiling:** 7/10 or 10/15
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="History (Ancient to Post-Independence)">
    **Syllabus Coverage:**
    - Ancient: IVC, Vedic, Mauryan, Guptas
    - Medieval: Delhi Sultanate, Mughals, Marathas
    - Modern: British Conquest, Socio-cultural Reform
    - Freedom Struggle: 1857-1947
    - Post-Independence: Integration, Reorganization

    **Non-Negotiables (6 marks total):**
    - **Contextual Specificity (2 marks)**: Samudragupta as Kaviraja, Iqta/Mansabdari, Acts (Pitts India Act), Associations
    - **Multi-Perspective Analysis (2 marks)**: 1857 as Mutiny vs First War, Subaltern perspectives, Women freedom fighters
    - **Spatial Context (1 mark)**: Empire boundaries, 1857 revolt locations, Dandi March route
    - **Cause-Effect-Legacy (1 mark)**: Battle of Buxar → Diwani rights → Economic transformation

    **Scoring Ceiling:** 7/10 or 10/15
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Society">

    - **Authoritative Data (2 marks)**: NFHS-5, Census 2011, PLFS, NCRB, Oxfam, Female LFPR, MPI
    - **Committees & Scholars (1.5 marks)**: Sachar, Mandal, Verma, Tendulkar/Rangarajan, M.N. Srinivas (Sanskritization)
    - **Constitutional Framework (1.5 marks)**: Articles 14, 15, 17, 21, 25-28, 44; Sabrimala, Vishaka, Navtej Johar, Puttaswamy cases
    - **Current Relevance (1 mark)**: COVID-19 on migrants, Digital divide in education

    **Scoring Ceiling:** 7/10 or 10/15

    - **Spatial Context (1 mark)**: Post-WW1 boundaries, Iron Curtain, German/Italian unification maps

## GS4 Rubrics (Ethics)

### Section-Based Evaluation

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Baseline Audit & Structure">
    **Non-Negotiables (6 marks total):**
    - **IBC Structure (2 marks)**: Introduction (context/definition), Body (sub-headings from keywords), Conclusion (value-based vision)
    - **Directive Word Mastery (1 mark)**: Discuss (360°), Critically Analyze (contradictions), Evaluate (judgment)
    - **Administrative Vibe (2 marks)**: Officer tone, administrative examples, district/field context
    - **Ceiling Awareness (1 mark)**: Understanding 0-20%, 21-45%, 46-60%, 61-70% bands

    **Scoring Ceiling:** 7/10 or 14/20
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Theory, Thinkers & Behavioral Logic">
    **Non-Negotiables (6 marks total):**
    - **Ethical Theory Triad (2 marks)**: Deontology (Kant), Teleology (Mill), Virtue Ethics (Aristotle); Gandhi, Vivekananda, Kautilya
    - **Attitude Models (2 marks)**: CAB Model, Katz's 4 Functions, Aristotle's Ethos-Pathos-Logos, Cialdini, Nudge Theory
    - **Leader Lessons (1 mark)**: Specific events (Mandela's forgiveness, Gandhi's Salt March), not just names
    - **Administrative Application (1 mark)**: Theory must solve the governance problem

    **Scoring Ceiling:** 7/10 or 14/20
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Values, EI & Probity">
    **Non-Negotiables (6 marks total):**
    - **Nolan Principles (2 marks)**: 7 Principles (Selflessness, Integrity, Objectivity, Accountability, Openness, Honesty, Leadership)
    - **Goleman's EI (2 marks)**: 5 Components (Self-awareness, Self-regulation, Motivation, Empathy, Social Skills)
    - **Probity Tools (1 mark)**: 2nd ARC 4th/10th Reports, Sevottam, Social Audit, RTI Section 4
    - **Code of Ethics vs Conduct (1 mark)**: Restrictive/External vs Aspirational/Internal

    **Scoring Ceiling:** 7/10 or 14/20
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Case Study Logic-Engine">
    **Non-Negotiables (10 marks total):**
    - **Topper Architecture (4 marks)**: Context intro, MANDATORY Stakeholder diagram, Ethical dilemmas (Value vs Value), Options with PESTEL
    - **Course of Action Quality (3 marks)**: Pragmatic + Legal + Spirit of Law + Short-term + Long-term
    - **Justification Anchors (2 marks)**: Constitutional Articles, Gandhi's Talisman, Rawls' Veil, Kant, Nolan
    - **Procedural vs Substantive Justice (1 mark)**: Distinction + actual civil servant examples

    **Scoring Ceiling:** 7/10 or 14/20
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Essay Rubrics

### 4-Section Framework (125 marks)

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Structure & Hook (30 marks)">
    **Topper Range:** 22-26 marks

    **Criteria:**
    - Hook Strategy: Anecdote/Storytelling vs Generic Definition
    - Problem Statement clarity in opening 150-200 words
    - Thesis Statement with roadmap by page 2
    - Paragraph Linking with Transitional Connectors
    - Full Circle Conclusion re-linking to opening
    - Temporal Leap to Vision 2047/SDG
    - Powerful, optimistic, Officer-like closing

    **Checklist:**
    - Intro Style: Anecdotal / Historical / Contemporary / Abstract
    - Thesis Clarity: High / Medium / Low
    - Flow Score: 1-10 (identify abrupt transitions)
    - Circularity Check: Re-linked to start?
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="PESTEL+ Multidimensionality (40 marks)">
    **Topper Range:** 30-35 marks

    **8 Dimensions (Mandatory Coverage):**
    - **P** - Political & Administrative
    - **E** - Economic
    - **S** - Social & Cultural (Gender MANDATORY)
    - **T** - Technological
    - **E** - Environmental
    - **L** - Legal & Ethical
    - **H** - Historical Context
    - **B** - Behavioral/Psychological

    **Checklist:**
    - Dimensions Covered: List all found
    - Missing Perspective: Most critical lens ignored
    - Data/Fact Density: High / Medium / Low
    - Spatial Scale: Individual → Family → Community → State → Nation → Global
    - Temporal Scale: Past → Present → Future
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Thematic Depth (35 marks)">
    **Topper Range:** 25-29 marks

    **Gold Dust Checklist:**
    - Constitutional Anchors: Articles 14, 15, 19, 21, 38, 44, 51A
    - Committees: 2nd ARC, Sachar, Mandal, Verma, Kasturirangan
    - Case Laws: Puttaswamy, Sabrimala, Vishaka, Bommai, Navtej Johar
    - Global Frameworks: COP26/Panchamrit, SDGs, Sendai, Paris Agreement
    - Scholars: Amartya Sen, Gandhi, Aristotle, Rousseau, Freud, Jung

    **Block-Specific Non-Negotiables:**
    - Block A (Philosophical): Psychological frameworks, cross-sectoral examples
    - Block B (Socio-Economic): NFHS-5, ASER, Constitutional Articles
    - Block C (Governance): 2nd ARC, Federal Logic, Legislative Frameworks
    - Block D (Environment & Science): COP outcomes, IPCC, Ecological philosophy
    - Block E (International): Soft Power, Global Commons, Institutions
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Expression & Logic (20 marks)">
    **Topper Range:** 14-17 marks

    **Criteria:**
    - Vocabulary Precision: Right word for context
    - Paragraph Rhythm: 100-150 words optimal
    - Tone: Balanced, Neutral, Constitutional (not political rant)
    - Bridge Sentences linking paragraphs
    - Core keyword mention every 2 pages
    - Logical Progression: Individual → Society → Nation → Global

    **Benchmarking:**
    - vs. Vikram Grewal: Philosophical depth, Poetry usage
    - vs. Gautam Vivekanandan: Data density, Constitutional flow
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

**Essay Ceiling:** 87/125 (70% cap)

## Scoring Matrix: Understanding the Bands

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Poor (0-30%)" icon="circle-xmark">

    - No data, articles, committees, or technical terms
    - Missing diagrams/maps (Geography, IR)
    - Purely descriptive, no analysis
  </Card>

  <Card title="Average (31-45%)" icon="circle-half-stroke">
    - Standard textbook definitions
    - Correct but lacks depth
    - Addresses demand partially
    - Missing sub-parts or non-negotiables
  </Card>

  <Card title="Good (46-60%)" icon="circle-check">
    - Uses ethical theories (Kant/Mill/Aristotle)
    - Cites 2nd ARC, Nolan Principles
    - Includes one administrative example
    - Mentions 1-2 articles/cases/data points
    - Decent structure with sub-headings
  </Card>

  <Card title="Topper (61-70%)" icon="trophy">
    - Precise vocabulary (Ethical Fading, Cognitive Dissonance)
    - Multi-dimensional PESTEL+ coverage
    - Exceptional diagrams (labeled, detailed)
    - High data density (specific % figures)
    - Constitutional Morality + Indian Ethos
    - Multiple committees with specific recommendations
    - Perfect map with legends (IR, Geography)
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Universal Rules Across All Subjects

<Note>
**The 70% Ceiling Rule**

The evaluator enforces a strict ceiling:
- 10-mark question: Maximum 7 marks
- 15-mark question: Maximum 10.5 marks
- 20-mark question: Maximum 14 marks
- 125-mark essay: Maximum 87 marks

This mirrors real UPSC marking where even Rank-1 toppers score 60-65%.
</Note>

<Warning>
**Generic Penalty**

Answers that are factually correct but lack Non-Negotiables (Data/Maps/Terms/Articles/Cases) are capped at 30% of total marks:
- 10-marker: Maximum 3 marks
- 15-marker: Maximum 4.5 marks
- 20-marker: Maximum 6 marks
</Warning>

## Category-Wise Scoring Breakdown

Every answer is evaluated across **4 main categories**:

### For GS1-3 (10/15-mark questions):

1. **Content & Relevance** (30-40% of marks)
   - Technical depth
   - Non-negotiables coverage
   - Factual accuracy

2. **PESTEL+ Multidimensionality** (20-30% of marks)
   - Multiple analytical lenses
   - Interconnections shown

3. **Visual Representation** (15-25% of marks)
   - Maps (MANDATORY for Geography, IR)
   - Diagrams, flowcharts
   - Process explanations

4. **Structure & Expression** (20-30% of marks)
   - IBC structure (Intro-Body-Conclusion)
   - Sub-headings from keywords
   - Demand adherence
   - Language quality

### For GS4 (10/20-mark questions):

1. **Theoretical Framework** (30-40% of marks)
   - Thinkers, theories, models
   - Nolan Principles, Goleman's EI

2. **Administrative Application** (30-40% of marks)
   - Real-world examples
   - District/field context
   - Officer-like tone

3. **Ethical Reasoning** (20-30% of marks)
   - Dilemma identification
   - Stakeholder mapping
   - PESTEL option analysis

4. **Constitutional Anchoring** (10-20% of marks)
   - Articles, Cases
   - Gandhi's Talisman
   - Value-based conclusions

### For Essays (125 marks):

See the 4-section framework above (30+40+35+20).
