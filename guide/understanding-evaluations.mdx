After submitting your answer, the AI evaluates it across multiple dimensions and provides detailed feedback to help you improve.

## Overall Score Card

The most prominent element is your **overall score**, displayed at the top:

<img src="/images/score-card.png" alt="Overall score card showing score, percentage, and summary" />

### Score Components

- **Numerical Score:** Your score out of the maximum marks (e.g., 7.5/10)
- **Percentage Bar:** Visual representation of your score as a percentage
- **Summary Statement:** A brief assessment of your performance

<Note>
  The overall score is calculated by summing your individual category scores.
</Note>

### Score Color Indicators

Scores are color-coded to help you quickly assess performance:

## Understanding the 70% Scoring Ceiling

<Warning>
  **Important Scoring Philosophy:**
  
  The JKPSC Answer Evaluator follows real UPSC/JKPSC evaluation standards where **70% is considered an excellent score**. It's extremely difficult to score above 70% in civil services mains exams.
  
  **Why this matters:**
  - A score of 60-70% indicates very good performance
  - Scores above 70% are rare even for top performers
  - Don't be discouraged by scores in the 60s — that's a strong result
  - Focus on consistent 60%+ scores across subjects
</Warning>

This ceiling is reflected in the Progress Dashboard charts, which show a reference line at 70%.

## Category Breakdown Tab

Click the **"Scores"** tab to see how you performed in each evaluation category:

### Evaluation Categories

Your answer is scored across multiple dimensions:

<Accordion title="Content Relevance & Coverage">
  **What it measures:** Whether you addressed the question directly and covered all required aspects.
  
  **Feedback examples:**
  - "Answer directly addresses the question and covers major dimensions"
  - "Missing discussion on economic impacts"
  - "Question demands critical analysis but answer is primarily descriptive"
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Understanding & Analytical Depth">
  **What it measures:** Your conceptual clarity and ability to analyze rather than merely describe.
  
  **Feedback examples:**
  - "Demonstrates strong analytical thinking with cause-effect linkages"
  - "Lacks critical evaluation of competing viewpoints"
  - "Good use of examples to illustrate concepts"
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Structure & Organization">
  **What it measures:** Logical flow, use of headings, introduction/conclusion, and overall presentation.
  
  **Feedback examples:**
  - "Well-structured with clear introduction and conclusion"
  - "Paragraphs are too long; use subheadings for better organization"
  - "Flow is disjointed; ideas not logically connected"
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Examples & Substantiation">
  **What it measures:** Use of facts, data, case studies, and real-world examples to support arguments.
  
  **Feedback examples:**
  - "Excellent use of recent examples (quota protests, judicial verdicts)"
  - "Arguments are theoretical; needs factual backing"
  - "Include specific article numbers and constitutional provisions"
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Conclusion & Way Forward">
  **What it measures:** Whether you provided a balanced conclusion and actionable suggestions.
  
  **Feedback examples:**
  - "Comprehensive way forward with administrative and policy recommendations"
  - "Conclusion is abrupt; doesn't synthesize the arguments"
  - "Good suggestions but lacks prioritization"
</Accordion>

### Reading Category Feedback

Each category shows:
- **Score bar:** Visual representation of category performance
- **Points awarded:** Fractional score out of maximum (e.g., 1.5/2)
- **Specific feedback:** Targeted advice on what worked and what needs improvement

## Key Points Analysis Tab

The **"Key Points"** tab shows which important points you covered and which you missed:

### Covered Points (Green Section)

These are the essential points you successfully included:

```markdown
✓ Discussed Article 356 and President's Rule provisions
✓ Mentioned the Sarkaria Commission recommendations
✓ Explained the floor test mechanism
```

### Missing Points (Red Section)

These are critical points you should have included:

```markdown
✗ Recent Supreme Court judgments on floor test timeline
✗ Role of anti-defection law (10th Schedule)
✗ Federal implications of Governor's discretion
```

<Tip>
  **How to use this section:**
  
  Review the missing points carefully and revise those topics. When you attempt similar questions in the future, ensure you include these dimensions.
</Tip>


These are areas where you're already performing well — maintain this quality in future answers.

## Improvement Tips Tab

The **"Tips"** tab provides actionable advice ranked by priority:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Priority 1">
    The most important improvement area, often related to fundamental issues like question interpretation or content coverage.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Priority 2">
    Secondary improvements that will strengthen your answer, such as better examples or analytical depth.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Priority 3+">
    Refinements for presentation, language, or advanced analytical techniques.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Example tips:**
```markdown
1. Include a brief introduction defining key terms before diving into analysis
2. Add more factual substantiation — cite specific cases, data, or committee reports
3. Use subheadings to break up long paragraphs and improve readability
4. Link your suggestions to SDG goals or current government initiatives
5. Practice writing crisp conclusions that synthesize arguments
```

<Note>
  Focus on implementing 1-2 tips at a time in your next answers rather than trying to fix everything at once.
</Note>

## Interpreting Scores Across Multiple Attempts

### Score Fluctuations Are Normal

Don't be surprised if your scores vary significantly:

- **Different question types** require different skill sets (analytical vs. descriptive)
- **Subject difficulty** varies (GS2 governance vs. GS3 technology)
- **Your familiarity** with the topic impacts performance

<Accordion title="What to do if scores drop">
  1. Review the category breakdown — which specific area declined?
  2. Check if you missed critical points due to lack of current affairs knowledge
  3. Compare feedback across attempts to identify patterns
  4. Don't panic over one low score — track trends over 5-10 evaluations
</Accordion>

### Focus on Category-Specific Improvement

Rather than obsessing over overall scores, track which categories consistently score low:

- **Low Structure scores?** Practice outlining before writing
- **Low Examples scores?** Build a current affairs database
- **Low Analytical Depth?** Read editorials and practice critical thinking
- **Low Coverage?** Work on question analysis and understanding keywords
