# General Studies 1: Evaluation Framework

GS1 covers Art & Culture, History (Ancient to Post-Independence), World History, Indian Society, and Geography. The evaluator uses a strict 70% ceiling with emphasis on technical terminology, visual representation, and PESTEL analysis.

## Mark Allocation

- **10-mark questions**: Max ceiling 7/10 (70%)
- **15-mark questions**: Max ceiling 10.5/15 (70%)
- **20-mark questions**: Max ceiling 14/20 (70%)

<Note>
In UPSC, 60-65% is considered Rank-1 level performance. The evaluator never exceeds 70% to maintain benchmark standards.
</Note>

## The Five Blocks

### Block 1: Art & Culture

**Topics Covered:**
- Indian Art Forms: Paintings (Mural, Miniature - Mughal/Rajput/Pahari Schools), Dances (Classical/Folk), Music (Hindustani/Carnatic), Puppetry, Pottery (OCP/BRW/PGW/NBPW)
- Visual Arts & Sculpture: Harappan, Mauryan, Post-Mauryan (Gandhara/Mathura/Amravati), Gupta, Medieval, Modern
- Architecture: Temple Styles (Nagara/Dravida/Vesara), Cave Architecture, Indo-Islamic, Colonial
- Literature: Vedic, Sanskrit, Buddhist/Jain, Sangam, Medieval, Modern
- Bhakti & Sufi Movements

**Non-Negotiables (6 marks total):**

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Technical Terminology (2 marks)">
    **Architecture Terms:**
    - Garbhagriha, Mandapa, Shikhara, Gopuram, Vimana, Antarala, Jagati
    - Indo-Islamic: Pietra Dura, Charbagh, Jali, Arabesque, Arcuate vs Trabeate
    
    **Painting Terms:**
    - Shadanga (Six limbs of painting)
    - Fresco-buono vs Fresco-secco
    - Gouache technique
    
    **Dance/Music Terms:**
    - Abhinaya, Lasya/Tandava
    - Alap, Gat, Thaat, Shruti
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Visual Representation & Maps (2 marks)">
    **Mandatory Requirements:**
    - At least one rough labeled sketch for Architecture or Sculpture
    - Cross-section of Stupa, Nagara temple floor plan, or comparison tables
    - Rough India map showing regional distribution of art forms
    - Location of major Mural sites or Temple style regions
    
    <Warning>
    No map/diagram = Maximum score capped at 4.5/10 or 7/15
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Chronological & Patronage Context (1 mark)">
    Must mention:
    - Dynasty or ruler responsible (Cholas for Bronzes, Chandellas for Khajuraho)
    - Material used (Red Sandstone-Mathura, Schist-Gandhara, Granite-Mahabalipuram)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Literature & Textual Sources (1 mark)">
    Reference to:
    - Natyashastra for dance
    - Vishnudharmottara Purana for paintings
    - Silappadikaram for Sangam culture
    - Ancient travelers' accounts (Megasthenes, Fa-Hien, Hiuen Tsang)
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

**PESTEL Dimensions:**

- **Socio-Religious**: How art reflected religious shifts (Hinayana to Mahayana transition, Bhakti movement influence)
- **Political**: Art as state propaganda (Ashokan Pillars, Mughal miniatures)
- **Economic**: Patronage sources (Guilds, Merchants, Royal patronage)
- **Environmental/Geographical**: Use of local materials (Terracotta in Bengal, Stone in Odisha, Wood in Kerala)

**Scoring Matrix:**

| Range | Marks (10) | Marks (15) | Description |
|-------|------------|------------|-------------|
| Poor | 1-2.5 | 1-4 | Purely descriptive, generic tourism-style writing, no technical terms |
| Average | 3-4.5 | 5-7 | Good understanding, sub-headings used, some technical terms, lacks diagram/map |
| Topper | 5-6.5 | 8-10 | Technical terminology precise, labeled diagram/map included, links art to socio-political history |

### Block 2: History (Ancient to Post-Independence)

**Topics Covered:**
- Ancient: Pre-historic, IVC, Vedic Society, Mahajanapadas, Mauryan, Post-Mauryan, Guptas, Harshavardhana, Southern Dynasties
- Medieval: Delhi Sultanate, Mughals, Maratha Empire, Deccan Sultanates
- Modern (Mid-18th to 1857): Regional powers, Carnatic Wars, British Conquest, Reform movements, Uprisings
- Freedom Struggle (1857-1947): Revolt of 1857, INC, Moderate/Extremist phases, Revolutionary activities, Gandhian Era
- Post-Independence: Princely States Integration, Linguistic Reorganization, Nehruvian Foreign Policy

**Non-Negotiables (6 marks total):**

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Contextual Specificity (2 marks)">
    **Ancient/Medieval:**
    - Specific titles (Samudragupta as Kaviraja)
    - Administrative units: Iqta, Mansabdari, Vishayas
    - Officials: Rajukas, Amatyas, Bhukti
    
    **Modern:**
    - Acts: Pitts India Act, Vernacular Press Act, Government of India Act 1935
    - Associations: Poona Sarvajanik Sabha, Indian Association
    - Journals/Books: Harijan, Hind Swaraj, Young India
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Multi-Perspective Analysis (2 marks)">
    **Different Historical Viewpoints:**
    - 1857: Sepoy Mutiny vs First War of Independence
    - Subaltern perspectives (Tribals, Peasants)
    - Women freedom fighters: Aruna Asaf Ali, Rani Gaidinliu, Kasturba Gandhi
    - Nationalist vs Imperialist vs Subaltern historiography
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Spatial Context & Maps (1 mark)">
    Required maps:
    - Empire extent (Mauryan/Mughal boundaries)
    - Movement centers (1857 revolt locations)
    - Dandi March route
    - IVC sites distribution
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Cause-Effect-Legacy (1 mark)">
    Must explain long-term impact:
    - Battle of Buxar → Diwani rights → Economic transformation
    - Constitutional legacy of freedom movement
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

**PESTEL Dimensions:**

- **Political/Administrative**: Centralized vs Decentralized governance, Law and Order systems
- **Economic**: Revenue systems (Zabti, Ryotwari, Mahalwari), Silk Route, Chola maritime trade, Deindustrialization
- **Social**: Status of women, caste dynamics, Orientalist vs Anglicist education debate
- **Technological/Military**: Gunpowder by Babur, Chola Naval technology, Railways/Telegraph introduction
- **Legal/Constitutional**: Charter Acts, Government of India Act 1935

### Block 3: World History

**Topics Covered:**
- Beginning of Modern World: Renaissance, Sea Routes Discovery, Reformation, Industrial Revolution
- Revolutions: American Revolution, French Revolution, European Nationalism
- World Wars: WWI (Russian Revolution, League of Nations), WWII (Fascism/Nazism, Holocaust)
- Decolonization: Cold War, Asia/Africa rise, Korean/Vietnam Wars, Soviet Collapse
- Political Philosophies: Communism, Capitalism, Socialism

**Non-Negotiables (6 marks total):**

- **Ideological & Philosophical Depth (2 marks)**: Correct definitions (Capitalism vs Socialism), link to Enlightenment thinkers (Rousseau, Voltaire, Montesquieu)
- **Global Inter-connectivity (2 marks)**: Link to India (Russian Revolution → Indian Freedom Struggle, American Civil War → Indian cotton farmers), Treaty mentions (Versailles, Yalta)
- **Spatial Context & Maps (1 mark)**: Post-WWI Europe boundaries, Iron Curtain, German/Italian unification maps
- **Structural Analysis of Change (1 mark)**: Industrial Revolution → Middle class rise → Feudalism decline

### Block 4: Indian Society

**Topics Covered:**
- Salient Features & Diversity: Caste, linguistic, religious diversity, Unity in Diversity, Family System
- Role of Women: 19th-century reforms, SHGs, Microfinance
- Population & Issues: Demographic Dividend, Population Aging
- Poverty & Development: Trickle-Down vs Inclusive Growth
- Urbanization: ULBs, Slums, Service Delivery
- Globalization: Glocalisation vs Homogenisation
- Social Empowerment: Communalism, Regionalism, Secularism

**Non-Negotiables (6 marks total):**

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Authoritative Data & Reports (2 marks)">
    **Must cite at least one:**
    - NFHS-5 data (Health, Fertility, Gender)
    - Census 2011 statistics
    - PLFS (Labour Force Participation)
    - NCRB (Crime statistics)
    - Oxfam (Inequality reports)
    - MPI (Multidimensional Poverty Index)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Committees, Scholars & Authors (1.5 marks)">
    - Sachar Committee (Minorities)
    - Mandal Commission (Caste)
    - Justice Verma Committee (Women)
    - Tendulkar/Rangarajan (Poverty)
    - Xaxa Committee (Tribals)
    - M.N. Srinivas: Sanskritization
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Constitutional & Legal Framework (1.5 marks)">
    **Articles:**
    - 14, 15, 17 (Untouchability), 21 (Life/Privacy)
    - 25-28 (Secularism), 44 (UCC)
    - 73rd/74th Amendment (ULBs)
    
    **Case Laws:**
    - Sabrimala Case (Secularism/Gender)
    - Vishaka Guidelines (Women)
    - Navtej Johar (LGBTQ/Diversity)
    - Puttaswamy (Privacy)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Critical Analysis & Current Relevance (1 mark)">
    Link static issues to current events:
    - COVID-19 impact on migrant laborers
    - Digital divide in education
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Block 5: World & Indian Geography

**Topics Covered:**
- World Physical: Geomorphology (Plate Tectonics, Rocks), Oceanography, Climatology, Soil Geography
- Geophysical Phenomena: Earthquakes, Tsunami, Volcanoes, Cyclones
- Natural Resources: Land, Forest, Water, Agriculture, Minerals, Energy
- Industrial Location: Primary/Secondary/Tertiary sector factors
- Indian Physical: Physiography, Drainage, Climate (Monsoon), Soils, Vegetation
- Human & Economic: Demography, Urbanization, Agriculture, Industry, Transport

**Non-Negotiables (6 marks total):**

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Visual Representation & Maps (2.5 marks)">
    **Mandatory for 5+ marks:**
    - India/World map for resource distribution or phenomena
    - Spatial distribution (Iron Ore, Cyclone tracks)
    - Technical process diagrams (Sea-floor spreading, Plate boundaries, Convection cells)
    - Labeled with proper legends
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Technical Terminology (1.5 marks)">
    - Albedo, Isostasy, Batholiths, Thermohaline Circulation
    - Orographic Lift, Xerophytes, Pedogenesis
    - Plate Tectonics, Continental Drift theories
    - Weber Model of Industrial Location
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Factors & Case Studies (1 mark)">
    - Industrial location: Raw Material vs Market-linkage logic
    - Recent examples: Joshimath Subsidence, Turkey Earthquake
    - Arctic Heatwaves, Monsoon anomalies
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Impact on Flora/Fauna (1 mark)">
    - Changes in critical geographical features
    - Effect on biodiversity
    - Climate change impacts on ecosystems
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### IBC Structure

All answers must follow the Introduction-Body-Conclusion framework:

**Introduction:**
- Definition, Data, or Current Context (e.g., "Recently in news...")

**Body:**
- Sub-headings derived from question keywords
- PESTEL dimensions applied
- Technical terminology and data integration

**Conclusion:**
- Forward-looking, solutions-oriented
- Linked to SDGs or Vision 2047

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

<Warning>
**Generic Penalty**: If an answer is correct but lacks Non-Negotiables (Data/Maps/Terms), it cannot score more than 3.5/10.
</Warning>

1. **Tourism-style writing**: Purely descriptive without analysis
2. **Missing maps/diagrams**: Visual representation is mandatory for 5+ marks
3. **Single-dimension answers**: Must cover multiple PESTEL lenses
4. **No data/statistics**: Must cite specific reports and figures
5. **Ignoring subaltern perspectives**: Include women, tribals, peasants in historical analysis

## Topper Benchmarks

**Ishita Kishore Style:**
- High fact density
- Multiple flowcharts
- Contextual intros

**Abhishek Style:**
- Technical maps with precise labeling
- Clear subheadings
- Specific committee mentions

## Examples of Expected Content

### Temple Architecture Question

**What evaluators expect:**
- Technical terms: Garbhagriha, Shikhara, Mandapa, Vimana
- Labeled cross-section diagram of Nagara temple
- Comparison table: Nagara vs Dravida vs Vesara
- Patronage context: Chandelas (Khajuraho), Cholas (Thanjavur)
- Material specificity: Granite in South, Sandstone in North
- Socio-religious link: Temple as economic hub and religious center

### Indian Monsoon Question

**What evaluators expect:**
- Technical terms: ITCZ, Tropical Easterly Jet, Sub-Tropical Westerly Jet, Orographic Lift
- Labeled map showing SW and NE monsoon wind directions
- Cross-section showing orographic rainfall in Western Ghats
- El Nino/La Nina impact explanation
- IMD data on monsoon arrival/departure
- Economic link: Monsoon dependence of agriculture, GDP impact

### Freedom Struggle Question

**What evaluators expect:**
- Multiple perspectives: Nationalist vs Imperialist vs Subaltern
- Specific movements: NCM (1920-22), CDM (1930-34), QIM (1942)
- Women's role: Sarojini Naidu, Aruna Asaf Ali, Kasturba Gandhi
- Constitutional legacy: Government of India Act 1935 provisions
- Map: Dandi March route or 1857 revolt centers
- Long-term impact analysis
