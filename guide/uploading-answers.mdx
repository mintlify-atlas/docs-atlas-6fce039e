The JKPSC Answer Evaluator accepts both PDF documents and image files. The AI will automatically extract the question and your answer from the uploaded content.

## Supported File Formats

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="PDF Documents" icon="file-pdf">
    Single PDF file containing your answer sheet
    - Max file size: **10MB**
    - Can include multiple pages
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Images" icon="image">
    Multiple images (JPG, PNG, or WebP)
    - Max file size: **10MB per image**
    - Upload multiple pages as separate images
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Warning>
  **Important:** Do not mix PDFs and images in a single upload. Choose either a PDF or images, not both together.
</Warning>

## How to Upload Files

<Steps>
  <Step title="Prepare Your Answer Sheet">
    Ensure your answer sheet includes both:
    - **The question** (typically printed/typed in a box or bordered area)
    - **Your handwritten answer** (the full response you want evaluated)
    
    <Note>
      Each upload should contain **one question and one answer**. The AI needs both to perform an accurate evaluation.
    </Note>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Choose Your Upload Method">
    You have two options:
    
    **Option 1: Drag and Drop**
    - Drag your PDF or image files directly onto the upload area
    - The border will highlight when files are ready to drop
    
    **Option 2: Click to Browse**
    - Click anywhere in the upload area
    - Select files from your device's file picker
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm Your Upload">
    After selecting files, you'll see:
    - The filename and size for PDF uploads
    - A preview grid for multiple images (with reordering option)
    
    Click the **"Process with AI"** button to begin extraction.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Uploading Multiple Image Pages

When uploading multiple images (e.g., answer sheets spanning several pages), you can reorder them before processing:

<Accordion title="How to reorder images">
  1. After selecting multiple images, a preview grid appears
  2. **Drag and drop** images to reorder them into the correct sequence
  3. **Remove** any unwanted images using the X button on each thumbnail
  4. Click **"Process X images with AI"** when ready
</Accordion>

<Tip>
  **The AI is smart about page order:** Even if images are uploaded out of order, the AI attempts to identify the correct reading sequence by looking for page numbers, "contd." markers, and logical content flow.
</Tip>

## Understanding the AI Extraction Process

After you upload your files, the AI performs several steps:

### Step 1: File Processing

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="PDF Files">
    The system first attempts to extract text directly from the PDF:
    - If sufficient text is found (≥50 characters), it proceeds to AI extraction
    - If the PDF is image-based or text extraction fails, it converts each page to an image (up to 10 pages)
    
    You'll see a progress indicator: **"Converting pages: Page X of Y"**
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Image Files">
    Images are converted to base64 format for transmission to the Gemini API:
    - The conversion happens for each image
    - Progress is shown: **"Converting images: X of Y"**
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Step 2: AI Extraction

The Gemini AI analyzes your content to:

1. **Identify the question** by looking for:
   - Printed or typed text in a box/border
   - Directive sentences ("Discuss...", "Evaluate...", "Critically examine...")
   - Mark allocations ("10 marks", "[15]")

2. **Extract your answer** by:
   - Reading all handwritten content
   - Preserving structure (headings, bullet points, numbered lists)
   - Recognizing diagrams, tables, and maps
   - Excluding word counts and struck-through text

<Note>
  The extraction process typically takes **10-30 seconds** depending on the number of pages and content complexity.
</Note>

## Reviewing Extracted Content

Once extraction is complete, you'll see two editable text areas:

### Extracted Question
- Shows the identified question text
- Click to edit if the AI missed anything or needs correction

### Extracted Answer
- Shows your complete answer with preserved formatting
- Displays word count at the bottom
- Click to edit if needed

<Warning>
  **If the answer appears empty:**
  
  You'll see a warning: "⚠ Answer appears empty — paste or type the answer below to enable evaluation"
  
  This can happen if:
  - The handwriting is very difficult to read
  - The image quality is too low
  - The PDF is encrypted or protected
  
  You can manually type or paste the answer to proceed with evaluation.
</Warning>

## Special Content Recognition

The AI recognizes and preserves special content:

<Accordion title="Diagrams & Visual Elements">
  When your answer includes diagrams, the AI inserts descriptive tags:
  
  - **Diagrams:** `[DIAGRAM: Flowchart showing anti-defection process under 10th Schedule]`
  - **Tables:** `[TABLE: 3×2 — comparing Speaker's role in Lok Sabha vs State Assemblies]`
  - **Maps:** `[MAP: Outline map of India marking states with anti-defection cases]`
  - **Timelines:** `[TIMELINE: Floor test controversies 2018–2023]`
  
  These tags tell the evaluator that you included visual elements in your answer.
</Accordion>

## Common Upload Issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title='"File is too large" error'>
    Each file must be under 10MB. Try:
    - Compressing your PDF using online tools
    - Reducing image resolution before uploading
    - Splitting multi-page PDFs into smaller sections
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title='"File format not supported" error'>
    Only these formats are accepted:
    - PDF: `.pdf`
    - Images: `.jpg`, `.jpeg`, `.png`, `.webp`
    
    Convert other formats (like HEIC or BMP) to supported types before uploading.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title='"Please add your Gemini API key first" error'>
    You must configure your API key before uploading files. See [Setting Up Your Account](/guide/setup).
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="AI extraction failed">
    If extraction fails:
    1. Check that your files contain readable text/images
    2. Verify your API key is valid
    3. Try uploading a smaller PDF (reduce to ≤10 pages)
    4. Manually enter the question and answer if automatic extraction fails
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Starting an Evaluation

After reviewing the extracted content:

1. Optionally select a **subject** (GS1, GS2, GS3, GS4, or Essay) from the dropdown
2. Set the **marks** for the question (defaults to 10)
3. Click the **"Evaluate Answer"** button

The AI will begin the evaluation process, which takes 10-20 seconds. You'll see a loading indicator with the message: **"AI is evaluating your answer..."**

<Tip>
  Want to evaluate another answer? Click the **X** button next to the filename to clear the current upload and start fresh.
</Tip>
